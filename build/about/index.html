What is VCP?

The Value Context Protocol enables privacy-preserving context sharing between humans, AI systems, and platforms.

VCP is a standardized format for encoding your preferences, constraints, and values in a way that platforms can understand — while keeping your private reasons private.

The Problem

Privacy Erosion

To get personalized experiences, you currently have to expose your personal details. Platforms know why you need accommodations, not just that you need them.

Context Fragmentation

Every platform asks the same questions. Your preferences, constraints, and goals are scattered across dozens of systems that don't talk to each other.

Opaque AI

AI systems make decisions about you based on context you can't see or control. There's no standard way to share your values and preferences with AI.

The VCP Solution

Privacy by Design

Share flags, not details. Platforms see "noise_restricted: true" not "lives in thin-walled apartment with noise-sensitive elderly neighbors."

Portable Context

Configure once, use everywhere. Your VCP context travels with you across compatible platforms, eliminating repetitive setup.

Full Transparency

See exactly what each platform receives. Audit trails show what was shared, what was withheld, and how private context influenced decisions.

Bilateral Alignment

VCP enables AI systems to share their own context, preferences, and constraints. This creates genuine two-way communication — alignment built with AI, not imposed upon it.

How It Works

1

Define Your Context

Build your VCP context with goals, preferences, constraints, and values using any compatible tool. Mark what's public, what requires consent, and what's always private.

→
2

Encode as Token

Your context becomes a compact CSM-1 token with emoji shortcodes. Private details become boolean flags that influence without exposing.

→
3

Share Selectively

Platforms receive only what you've consented to share. Different stakeholders see different views of the same context.

→
4

Audit Everything

Full audit trail of what was shared, when, and with whom. You always know how your context influenced decisions.
